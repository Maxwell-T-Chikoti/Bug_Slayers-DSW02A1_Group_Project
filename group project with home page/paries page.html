Other Contesting Parties & Candidates

ACDP

The African Christian Democratic Party (ACDP) is a South African political party founded in 1993. It is a conservative Christian party based on Biblical principles. The leader of the party is Kenneth Meshoe. READ MORE

President: Kenneth Meshoe

Al-Jama-ah (ALJAMA AH)

The Al Jama-ah Political Party was established in 2007 by its Party Leader, Ganief Hendricks, with the aim of contributing positively to the transformation of post-Apartheid South Africa. READ MORE

President: Ganief Hendricks

AIC

The African Independent Congress (AIC) is a minor political party in South Africa founded in Matatiele on 12 December 2005. It was formed in protest against the location of the area within the boundaries of the Eastern Cape province rather than KwaZulu-Natal. READ MORE

President: Mandla Galo

African Transformation Movement (ATM)

The African Transformation Movement (ATM) is a political party in South Africa led by Vuyolwethu Zungula. It was formed with the backing of the South African Council of Messianic Churches in Christ (SACMCC). READ MORE

President: Vuyolwethu Zungula

COPE

The Congress of the People (COPE) is a South African political party formed in 2008 by former members of the African National Congress (ANC). The party was founded by former ANC members Mosiuoa Lekota, Mbhazima Shilowa, and Mluleki George to contest the 2009 general election. READ MORE

President: Mosiuoa Lekota

IFP

The Inkatha Freedom Party (IFP) is a right-wing political party in South Africa founded by Mangosuthu Buthelezi in 1975. While primarily based in KwaZulu-Natal, it has contested elections across the country. READ MORE

President: Velenkosini Hlabisa
